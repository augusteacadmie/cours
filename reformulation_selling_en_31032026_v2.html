Unit 4 · Taking Orders · English

Reformulation as a sales tool —
the natural link

EN

Three techniques · Click each example to reveal the full exchange

Reformulation as a proposal — When the order is open or generic, the reformulation becomes the recommendation itself. You answer the guest's need with a specific proposal, framed as a natural confirmation. No selling — just completing.
🌿

Example 1

"Something light and refreshing."

▾
Guest

"I'm not sure — something light, not too sweet, refreshing."

You

"In that case — I'd suggest our Cucumber Cooler. It's gin, fresh cucumber, tonic and a touch of elderflower. Very clean, not sweet at all. Does that sound good?"

Why it works : The guest gave you the brief. You turned it into a product. The reformulation replaces the hesitation — the guest doesn't need to decide, they just need to say yes.

🥃

Example 2

"A gin and tonic."

▸
Guest

"I'll have a gin and tonic, please."

You

"Of course — do you have a preference on the gin, or shall I suggest something? We have the Hendrick's at the moment — very floral, with cucumber notes. It's just two euros more than the house gin. Shall I go with that?"

Why it works : The order was complete — but it left the brand open. That gap is the upgrade opportunity. The price difference is named immediately after the value. The guest decides freely, with full information.

☕

Example 3

"Just a coffee, thanks."

▸
Guest

"We've finished — just a coffee to close, please."

You

"Of course. If you'd like something to go alongside — we have a Calvados Pierre Huet, aged eight years. It pairs beautifully with coffee — warm, rounded, with a touch of apple. A classic French way to close a meal."

Why it works : The addition is framed as a pairing, not a sale. "To go alongside" signals coherence, not upselling. The cultural reference adds value without pressure.

🍋

Example 4

"Something for my wife — she doesn't drink much."

▸
Guest

"And for my wife — she doesn't really drink, but something nice, you know?"

You

"In that case — our Yuzu Spritz is a wonderful choice. Very light on alcohol, yuzu citrus, elderflower and sparkling water. It's beautiful to look at and very easy to drink. I think she'll love it."

Why it works : The brief came from a third party. You answered the actual need — something beautiful and approachable — not just the constraint. "I think she'll love it" closes with genuine warmth, not a sales line.

✦

The rule : reformulation becomes a sales tool the moment you stop repeating the order and start completing it. A name, a detail, a pairing suggestion — woven into the confirmation, never added on top of it.
